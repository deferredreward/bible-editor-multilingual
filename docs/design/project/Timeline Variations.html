Screen B — columns: ULT only

Click the columns icon and you flip into a single-column doc view. ULT reads top-to-bottom like a Word document; you can place the cursor anywhere and just type. Verse numbers carry a small ⌭ alignment icon. Active verse is softly highlighted; click any verse to make it active. The resource column to the right keeps notes etc. for whichever verse you're on.

  • Continuous-prose editing — best when you're rewriting flow across verses.
  • Alignment one tap away per verse via ⌭ icon.
  • UST and UHB are out of sight here — flip back / add columns when needed.

Screen C — columns: ULT, UST, UHB

Three parallel doc columns. ULT and UST are editable Word-style; UHB is read-only. The right resource panel collapses narrower (drag the divider to give scripture more room — needed when 3 columns are up). Verse-by-verse alignment is row-locked across columns so your eye can track horizontally.

  • True side-by-side editing for ULT/UST; UHB always visible for source.
  • Resource panel can be narrowed/widened to taste.
  • Narrower columns — better on wide screens.

Screen D — alignment dialog with verse context

When you click the ⌭ on a verse, the alignment editor opens as a modal. Unlike today's modal, it carries a verse strip at the top: the current verse's ULT and UST shown side-by-side, so you can read the surrounding meaning as you drag tokens. Left rail: unaligned GL words drag onto Hebrew boxes on the right.

  • Never lose the meaning of the verse while word-aligning.
  • UST sometimes makes a fuzzy alignment obvious.
  • Modal still hides the rest of the chapter — but Esc returns you exactly where you were.
