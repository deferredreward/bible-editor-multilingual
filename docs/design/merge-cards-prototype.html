Why these, and not the corner grip

The first sketch's grip + the select-ring both landed in the top-right corner — directly under the Hebrew occurrence superscript (כָל² on the middle card). That corner is spoken for. Your existing × already dodges this by living top-left, in the empty zone of the right-aligned Hebrew row. So a whole-card handle has to avoid top-right too. All three below do.

Left rail ⋮ (recommended visible handle)

A vertical grip down the left edge — the one card region that's neither draggable content nor the × corner. Bonus: it replaces the compound spine you already draw on the left, so it carries double duty (teal dots = compound, grab to merge). Always visible, so it's discoverable; collision-free.

Bottom grip ⠿

A small grip pill centered below the chips. Also collision-free and unambiguous, but it's further from the eye and the card's height shifts it around as chips wrap.

Hold-Alt + drag

No chrome at all — the whole card body drags while Alt is held (without Alt you'd drag a chip). Cleanest visually, zero collision, but invisible/undiscoverable on its own and dead on touch.

Is a single-word card any different? (no)

To the user: no — a card is a card. Drag any card's grip onto another and it merges the same way, whether it holds one Hebrew word or five. The grip sits on every card, so there's no exception to learn.

Under the hood it's even nicer: a single-word-card merge is literally your existing "drag the Hebrew word" op (moveSource). When a group has only one source word, moving it out collapses the group and its English follows to the destination — exactly a full card merge. The whole-card grip is just that same move generalized to all the card's Hebrew words at once.

They only diverge on multi-word cards: dragging a single Hebrew word peels off just that word (the English stays put unless it was the last), while dragging the whole card by its grip always takes everything — all Hebrew + all chips. The single-word card (see אָלָה → curse, far right) is simply the case where those two gestures coincide.

Where this leaves us

Bottom grip it is. mergeGroups(state, survivor, eaten) ≈ moveSource looped over the eaten card's source words (English re-points automatically on the final collapse). Keep the one-tap Undo (= clearGroup split) so the gesture stays low-stakes.
